# Blog Strategy

Why the blog exists, who it is for, and how the topic mix is balanced. Open this when deciding *what* to write next or *where* a post should land. Per-post operational spec is in [BLOG.md](BLOG.md). Brand identity and voice principles are in [BRAND.md](BRAND.md).

## 1. Brand positioning

> **Primary aim**: build personal authority that converts to AI-consulting inbound, with LinkedIn as the engagement channel and the MENA / Arabic-OTT niche as the defensible positioning angle.

**Why this over alternatives:**

- Recruiter signals come free from strong thought leadership. No separate strategy required.
- Pure "authority without commercial endpoint" produces nice essays that feed nothing. The planned AI-consulting / course business needs a top-of-funnel.
- Pure "lead-gen without a niche" forces competition against every Databricks consultant on Earth. MENA / Arabic-OTT data and AI work is defensible because delivery experience here is rare.
- LinkedIn over SEO because B2B AI consulting in MENA happens through warm intros and trust, not Google search.

**Practical implications:**

- Inbound is handled by the contact page and home Hero, not by a per-post CTA block. Posts end at the closing question.
- MENA / Arabic-OTT framing is structural in posts about delivered work, not background colour.
- LinkedIn is the engagement channel. No posting workflow exists today; tracked under "LinkedIn share + OG image generation flow" in [../TODO.md](../TODO.md) Future enhancements.
- No newsletter on the portfolio. The AI business site, when it launches, is the natural home for an owned email list.
- The current employer is on the CV, not the subject of any post. Posture and rationale live in [BLOG.md section 10](BLOG.md#10-confidentiality).

## 2. Content pillars

Two delivery pillars and one reflection lane. Pillars are a mental model for "what is underweight, write that next." They are not in frontmatter and never have been; they live here only.

### Delivery pillars

The work the blog is evidence of. The pillar-balance argument applies between these two.

- **`data-foundations`**: semantic layers, KPI governance, data modeling, BI platform modernization, pipeline reliability and refresh operations. The layer that makes AI possible. Governance is the wedge for consulting engagements (nobody hires you for AI if their data layer is on fire), and the bucket also covers adjacent foundations work that does not foreground governance.
- **`applied-ai`**: production AI that ships. Voice-of-customer NLP, CRM automation, audience enrichment, GenAI / RAG / agent workflows running daily on a real platform. The differentiator from generic BI consultants.

### Reflection lane

- **`bi-to-ai`**: how analytics organizations actually evolve. Career arc, BI-to-DS transitions, role evolution, organizational data maturity, leadership perspective on team design. Format and intent are different from the delivery pillars; these post when there is a genuine reflection to surface, not on a cadence.

### Current balance

Roughly 10 / 3 / 3 across `data-foundations` / `applied-ai` / `bi-to-ai`. `applied-ai` is the thinnest relative to its strategic importance. Future delivery posts should weight toward `applied-ai` until parity.
