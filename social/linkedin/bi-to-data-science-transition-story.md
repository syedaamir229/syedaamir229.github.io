One Friday, an hour apart: a dashboard reading zero, and a model returning nulls for half its profiles. Same engineer, same tables underneath, two stakeholders both waiting on me.

That ranking, which side gets the next two hours, is where most BI-to-data-science moves quietly fall apart. The instinct is to call one side legacy and walk away from it. That instinct is the mistake.

The trust your dashboards earned over years is the same trust that lets your first model land without an argument. Spend it too early and the model ships straight into a credibility gap.

Three things end the transition: a parallel ML data model, dropping BI too soon, and treating accuracy as the only thing that counts.

I wrote up the six-month overlap that avoids all three.
