Most data models are scoped to die, and the kickoff meeting is where it happens.

They get funded by a familiar scene: two teams, two defensible versions of the same number, twenty minutes spent deciding which one goes on the slide. Then someone calls the fix a "project." That word is the whole problem. A project ships a clean schema and ends, and the next consumer quietly rebuilds the foundation underneath it. Badly. So does the one after that.

Infrastructure is the other path: one foundation that carries the semantic layer, the feature store, the automation, and eventually the AI agent, without anyone rebuilding it.

Five rules decide which one you end up with. Three of them, grain, movement, and feature tables, can't be retrofitted cheaply, so they have to land in v1.
