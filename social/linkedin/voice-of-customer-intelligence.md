Launch morning, a product manager asks for sentiment on last night's premiere. The answer lands three days later, hand-pulled from four platforms and sample-translated from Arabic, by which point the team has already moved to the next title.

The data existed before the question was asked. The pipeline to surface it didn't.

A five-layer stack closes that gap: ingestion from four platforms, NLP enrichment (translation, sentiment, and the URL-to-title join that quietly makes the rest possible), a semantic model, two specialised SQL agents behind a supervisor, and a workspace to ask in plain language.

The decision the whole thing turned on was splitting one agent into two. The post is mostly about why that mattered.
