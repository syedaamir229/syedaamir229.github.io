A churn model and a churn dashboard disagreed on a few thousand profiles, and an executive asked the obvious question: which one is right.

Both were. The model used a 30-day inactivity window, the dashboard used a billing-cycle definition. Defensible, different, and now a list of real customers was in dispute.

BI and data science drift into separate versions of reality unless you connect them on purpose. Four moves keep them honest: shared entities, careful feature promotion, validating for use instead of just for a score, and letting the roles evolve rather than fork.

The one that does the heavy lifting is shared entities. Same governed definitions on both sides, and the disagreement simply can't form.
