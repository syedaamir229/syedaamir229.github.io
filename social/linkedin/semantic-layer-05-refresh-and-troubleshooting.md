Monday standup, the executive dashboard goes up, and yesterday's row is blank. The overnight refresh failed and nobody saw the alert.

That's the version of a refresh failure that actually hurts: leadership notices before the data team does, and the afternoon review runs on stale numbers while someone backfills live in front of an audience.

The way out is to stop treating refresh as something only one person understands. Write it down. A six-stage refresh sequence, and a five-step recovery for the missed day, so anyone on the team can run it without guessing.
