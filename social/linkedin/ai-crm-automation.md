A CRM team asked for a same-day audience on a Monday. The analyst quoted three days. By the time it was ready, the promotion had run and the moment was gone.

Every campaign started life as a ticket. Every ticket was basically last month's ticket with a new title. Every audience got rebuilt from scratch, because nothing from the last one was reusable. The usual response is to add people.

The better one is to delete the loop. A six-layer operating system turns those handoffs into configuration: data foundation, scenario engine, identity resolution, prioritisation, CRM integration, and a feedback loop that actually closes. Operators target their own audiences, and the data team steps off the critical path.
