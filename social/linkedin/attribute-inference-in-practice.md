A content team wanted to know what the female audience over 25 actually watches. The problem: only about a quarter of adult profiles had ever stated a gender. The rest was blank.

A year later that same question had a real answer across millions of profiles, most of them predictions on rows that started with nothing.

The danger with inferred data is the moment it leaves the model and gets treated as fact. Four guardrails decide whether it helps or quietly does harm: how you design the features, validation that doesn't flatter itself, explicit labels in every downstream contract, and drift monitoring that accounts for context.

Underneath all four is one rule: a prediction should never travel without its confidence flag.
