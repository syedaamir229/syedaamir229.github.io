A semantic layer can be technically perfect on Monday and quietly bypassed by Friday.

The reason is boring. A local measure takes an analyst five minutes; a model change takes two weeks. So people route around the model, and your single source of truth becomes one source among several.

That's a governance problem wearing an engineering costume. The programs that fail tend to hit the same four traps: engineering-first, big-bang migration, orphan KPIs, and the deployment cliff.

What breaks all four is doing the unglamorous part first. Six weeks mapping the 20 to 30 KPIs that cause the most reconciliation, before anyone picks a tool. Scope shrinks, ownership gets obvious, and the first release ships while people still care.
