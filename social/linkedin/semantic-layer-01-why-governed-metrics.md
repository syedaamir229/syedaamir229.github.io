Three teams, three different counts of "active subscribers," all off the same source data. Trial users in or out. Profile or account. Twenty minutes of the meeting gone before anyone reaches the decision they came to make.

The reflex is to go shopping for a semantic-layer tool. The honest first move is to admit the first six weeks aren't a tooling problem at all. They're a disagreement problem. Settle the definitions before you choose the platform, or you'll just encode the disagreement and ship it everywhere.

This kicks off a six-part series on building a semantic layer that actually holds up inside a real org.
